Damping

Types of Damping

Light Damping
  • Light damping is when the amplitude of each oscillation decreases exponentially.
  • The time period of each oscillation stays constant through each cycle.
Critical Damping
  • Critical damping returns the system back to the equilibrium position without overshooting (going past the equilibrium position like light damping does).
  • Critical damping damps oscillations in the shortest time possible.
Heavy Damping
  • Heavy damping returns the object to the equilibrium position very slowly.
  • No oscillating motion occurs.
  • Heavy damping is any damping that is slower than critical damping.
  • Depending on how heavy the damping is, the object may never return to the equilibrium position.

Resonance

  • For a system with little or no damping, when the frequency of the applied periodic force is equal to the natural frequency, resonance occurs.
  • This results in maximum energy transfer, and maximum amplitude oscillations.
